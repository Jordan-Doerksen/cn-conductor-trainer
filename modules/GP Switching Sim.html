A conductor's yard: line the switches, call the move, watch the engineer work it.
Amber tick = clearance point — keep cuts clear of the switch. Solid car = loaded, hollow = empty · kick limit 5 empties / 3 loaded (113.5 special instruction).
An unofficial personal study aid — the official CROR and CN special instructions govern. Securing, coupling and kicking modeled on Rules 112, 113 & 115.

How to play

You're the conductor, not the engineer. You don't drive — you line the road and call the move, then the engineer works it. Get the cars where the job calls for them.

1 · Line the switches

Tap a switch on the yard (or its chip below) to throw it normal (stay on the ladder) or reverse (into that track). To reach a track you line its switch reverse and leave the switches between it and the lead normal so you can roll past them. Call a move into a track that isn't lined and the engineer stops — just like real life.

Restore switches to normal. When you're done with a switch, line it back to normal. Main-track switches must be left lined and locked normal (CROR 104(h), except when the track's occupied or a form authorizes it) — and it's good practice in the yard too. You also can't throw a switch with cars standing across the points (104(c)) — that's the clearance point again.

2 · Call the move

PULL n from a track — back in, couple, pull the cars out onto the lead. +1 joint (you coupled on).
SPOT n to a track — shove cars in and leave them. Spotting 2+ ties the cut down (hand brake on) — now it can take a kick. +1 joint if you couple onto cars already there.
KICK n to a track — shove and cut away rolling; the cars coast on by themselves onto a secured 2+ cut on a kickable track. no joint. Kicking is capped at 5 empties / 3 loaded at once (113.5 special instruction); solid car = loaded, hollow = empty.

Don’t foul the switch. Leave every cut clear of its switch — the amber tick on each track is the clearance point. Park a car closer than that and it fouls the route into the next track, so the engineer won’t leave it there. Same on the lead — pull back far enough to clear the points.

The rules it follows — and why

  • 112 — securing: every cut you leave gets a hand brake; a 2+ cut is then tied and kickable.
  • 113.4 / 113.5 — kicking: only onto a secured standing cut, never an empty or short track.
  • 113.5 special instruction — kick at most 5 empties or 3 loaded at once.
  • 114 — fouling other tracks: every cut left standing must be clear of the clearance point.
  • 115 — shoving: you line and protect the route before the move.

A training aid; the official CROR governs. Flag anything that looks off.
